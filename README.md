# Recursive-Entropy-Systems-and-the-Architecture-of-Entropic-Governance
McPhetridge’s REM-Evo framework uses recursive logic to test and prune theories. Prioritizing structural survival over metaphysics via a bridge language, it externalizes cognition into AI to prove knowledge and authorship survive only under maximal eliminative stress.

Mitchell D. McPhetridge’s research develops a constraint-first framework designed to evaluate complex systems through recursive logic and adversarial testing. At its core is the REM-Evo runtime, a methodology that treats theories as disposable candidates to be rigorously pruned or killed if they fail to meet specific audit and falsification criteria. This approach prioritizes operational persistence and structural invariants over metaphysical truth, using a bridge language to safely translate concepts across different disciplines. McPhetridge further explores creator shadow and mutual observation, arguing that identity and authorship are emergent properties of intention rather than mere mechanical continuation. These technical systems are often externalized into modular AI architectures and semantic indexing tools to ensure the procedures can function independently of the author. Ultimately, the work advocates for systemic discipline where knowledge survives only by enduring maximal conceptual stress and eliminative pressure.
